## Privacy Policy

### ISRS Portal Privacy Policy

**Last Updated**: [DATE]

The International Shellfish Restoration Society ("ISRS", "we", "us", or "our") respects your privacy and is committed to protecting your personal information. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard your information when you use our unified portal.

#### 1. Information We Collect

**1.1 Information You Provide Directly**

When you register for an account or use our services, we may collect:

- **Contact Information**: Name, email address, phone number, mailing address
- **Professional Information**: Organization, position, department, research areas, expertise
- **Profile Information**: Biography, profile photo, CV/resume, publications
- **Social Media**: LinkedIn URL, Twitter handle, ORCID ID, personal website
- **Conference Information**: Registration details, dietary restrictions, accessibility needs
- **Payment Information**: Credit card details (processed by third-party payment processors), membership status
- **Submissions**: Abstracts, presentations, posters, photos, resources, white papers
- **Communications**: Messages you send through the portal, survey responses, feedback

**1.2 Information Collected Automatically**

- **Device Information**: IP address, browser type, operating system, device identifiers
- **Usage Data**: Pages visited, time spent on pages, links clicked, search queries
- **Session Information**: Login times, session duration, geographic location (approximate)
- **Cookies and Tracking**: See our [Cookie Policy](#cookie-policy)

**1.3 Information from Third Parties**

- **Payment Processors**: Stripe, PayPal (payment confirmation, transaction IDs)
- **Email Service**: SendGrid, AWS SES (delivery status, open rates, click rates)
- **Social Media**: Public profile information if you link your accounts
- **Conference Platforms**: Zoom, Whova (attendance, engagement data)

#### 2. How We Use Your Information

We use your personal information for the following purposes:

**2.1 Core Services**
- Provide access to the ISRS portal and member benefits
- Process conference registrations and membership applications
- Facilitate networking and professional connections
- Manage abstract submissions and reviews
- Coordinate field trips, special events, and workshops

**2.2 Communications**
- Send conference announcements and updates
- Deliver newsletters and educational content
- Respond to inquiries and support requests
- Send administrative notices (payment receipts, policy changes)
- Facilitate board communications (for board members only)

**2.3 Directory & Networking**
- Display your profile in the member directory (with your consent)
- Enable other members to find and connect with you
- Showcase sponsor and exhibitor information
- Highlight presenter and organizer contributions

**2.4 Analytics & Improvement**
- Analyze portal usage to improve user experience
- Monitor system performance and security
- Understand member demographics and research interests
- Evaluate conference success and attendance patterns

**2.5 Legal & Compliance**
- Comply with legal obligations and regulations
- Enforce our Terms of Service
- Protect against fraud and security threats
- Respond to legal requests and prevent harm

#### 3. Information Sharing & Disclosure

**3.1 With Your Explicit Consent**

We will share your personal information with third parties only when you explicitly consent:

| Data Category | Shared With | Purpose | Your Control |
|---------------|-------------|---------|--------------|
| Email Address | Conference Attendees (opt-in) | Networking | Directory opt-in toggle |
| Phone Number | Conference Organizers (opt-in) | Event logistics | Visibility setting |
| Resume/CV | Sponsors/Employers (opt-in) | Recruitment | CV visibility setting |
| Photos | Public website visitors | Showcase conferences | Photo approval system |
| Research Areas | All members | Professional networking | Profile visibility setting |

**3.2 With Service Providers**

We share limited information with trusted service providers who assist in operations:

- **Payment Processors** (Stripe, PayPal): Payment card info, transaction amounts
- **Email Service** (SendGrid, AWS SES): Email address, name (for transactional emails)
- **Cloud Hosting** (AWS, Render): All data (stored securely with encryption)
- **Analytics** (Google Analytics): Anonymized usage data
- **Communication Tools** (Zoom): Name, email for conference sessions

**Service Provider Requirements**:
- Must sign Data Processing Agreements
- Must comply with GDPR, CCPA, and privacy laws
- Must use data only for specified purposes
- Must implement appropriate security measures

**3.3 Legal Requirements**

We may disclose your information if required by law:

- In response to subpoenas, court orders, or legal processes
- To protect ISRS's legal rights and property
- To prevent fraud, security threats, or illegal activity

**3.4 Aggregated & De-Identified Data**

We may share anonymized, aggregated statistics that cannot identify individuals:

### Consent Audit Trail

Every consent action is logged:

```sql
CREATE TABLE consent_log (
  id UUID PRIMARY KEY DEFAULT uuid_generate_v4(),
  user_id UUID REFERENCES users(id),
  consent_type VARCHAR(100), -- 'directory_opt_in', 'email_newsletter', etc.
  consent_given BOOLEAN, -- true = granted, false = revoked
  consent_date TIMESTAMP DEFAULT NOW(),
  ip_address VARCHAR(45),
  user_agent TEXT,
  consent_version VARCHAR(20), -- version of privacy policy at time of consent
  expiry_date TIMESTAMP, -- for time-limited consents
  notes TEXT
);
```

**Audit Requirements**:
- Must prove consent was freely given
- Must show consent was informed (link to policy version)
- Must track when consent was obtained and by whom
- Must enable easy withdrawal of consent

4. **Legitimate Interests**: Fraud prevention, network security (with balancing test)

**ISRS Compliance Measures**:
- ✅ Consent management system (granular, easy to withdraw)
- ✅ Data subject rights (access, rectification, erasure, portability, objection)
- ✅ Privacy by design and default
- ✅ Data Protection Impact Assessments (DPIAs) for high-risk processing
- ✅ Data Processing Agreements with all processors
- ✅ Breach notification (72 hours to supervisory authority, without undue delay to individuals)
- ✅ EU representative appointed (if required)
- ✅ Data transfers safeguarded (Standard Contractual Clauses, adequacy decisions)

## User Rights & Data Protection

### How to Exercise Your Rights

**1. Access Your Data**
- **Self-Service**: Log into portal → Settings → Privacy → [Download My Data]
- **Format**: JSON or CSV
- **Contents**: All personal information, consents, activity logs
- **Timeline**: Instant download

**2. Correct Your Data**
- **Self-Service**: Edit profile directly
- **Request Assistance**: privacy@isrs.org
- **Timeline**: Immediate (self-service) or 7 days (staff assistance)

**3. Delete Your Data**
- **Self-Service**: Settings → Privacy → [Delete My Account]
- **Confirmation**: Sent to your email (click to confirm)
- **Effect**: Account deleted within 90 days, most data deleted within 30 days
- **Exceptions**: Financial records retained 7 years (legal requirement)

**4. Withdraw Consent**
- **Self-Service**: Privacy Dashboard → Toggle consents
- **Effect**: Immediate (may take 24 hours for some systems)
- **Consequences**: May limit services (e.g., opting out of directory)

**5. Object to Processing**
- **Email**: privacy@isrs.org
- **Specify**: Which processing you object to and why
- **Review**: We assess legitimate grounds and respond within 30 days

**6. Restrict Processing**
- **When**: While we investigate accuracy or lawfulness
- **Effect**: Data stored but not processed
- **Timeline**: Resolved within 30 days

**7. Data Portability**
- **Format**: Machine-readable JSON or CSV
- **Contents**: Data you provided (not derived data)
- **Transmit**: To another service (if technically feasible)

## Implementation Database Schema

### Consent Management Tables

```sql
-- Consent types (catalog of all consents)
CREATE TABLE consent_types (
  id UUID PRIMARY KEY DEFAULT uuid_generate_v4(),
  name VARCHAR(100) UNIQUE NOT NULL,
  category VARCHAR(50) NOT NULL, -- 'essential', 'directory', 'communication', 'data_sharing', 'analytics'
  description TEXT,
  is_required BOOLEAN DEFAULT false,
  default_value BOOLEAN DEFAULT false,
  version VARCHAR(20) NOT NULL,
  effective_date DATE NOT NULL,
  policy_url TEXT,
  created_at TIMESTAMP DEFAULT NOW()
);

-- User consents (tracking each user's consent status)
CREATE TABLE user_consents (
  id UUID PRIMARY KEY DEFAULT uuid_generate_v4(),
  user_id UUID REFERENCES users(id) ON DELETE CASCADE,
  consent_type_id UUID REFERENCES consent_types(id),
  consent_given BOOLEAN NOT NULL,
  consented_at TIMESTAMP DEFAULT NOW(),
  consent_method VARCHAR(50), -- 'web_form', 'email_link', 'event_registration'
  consent_version VARCHAR(20), -- version of policy at time of consent
  ip_address VARCHAR(45),
  user_agent TEXT,
  expires_at TIMESTAMP, -- for time-limited consents
  withdrawn_at TIMESTAMP,
  UNIQUE(user_id, consent_type_id)
);

-- Consent log (immutable audit trail)
CREATE TABLE consent_log (
  id UUID PRIMARY KEY DEFAULT uuid_generate_v4(),
  user_id UUID REFERENCES users(id),
  consent_type_id UUID REFERENCES consent_types(id),
  action VARCHAR(20) NOT NULL, -- 'granted', 'revoked', 'updated'
  consent_given BOOLEAN NOT NULL,
  timestamp TIMESTAMP DEFAULT NOW(),
  ip_address VARCHAR(45),
  user_agent TEXT,
  consent_version VARCHAR(20),
  notes TEXT
);

-- Data sharing agreements (with third parties)
CREATE TABLE data_sharing_agreements (
  id UUID PRIMARY KEY DEFAULT uuid_generate_v4(),
  party_name VARCHAR(255) NOT NULL,
  party_type VARCHAR(50) NOT NULL, -- 'sponsor', 'partner', 'service_provider'
  agreement_type VARCHAR(50) NOT NULL, -- 'DPA', 'sponsor_agreement'
  signed_date DATE NOT NULL,
  expiry_date DATE,
  data_categories TEXT[], -- ['email', 'name', 'organization']
  purposes TEXT[], -- ['networking', 'recruitment']
  agreement_url TEXT,
  contact_person VARCHAR(255),
  contact_email VARCHAR(255),
  is_active BOOLEAN DEFAULT true,
  created_at TIMESTAMP DEFAULT NOW()
);

-- Data processing activities (GDPR Article 30 requirement)
CREATE TABLE processing_activities (
  id UUID PRIMARY KEY DEFAULT uuid_generate_v4(),
  activity_name VARCHAR(255) NOT NULL,
  controller VARCHAR(255) NOT NULL, -- 'ISRS'
  processor VARCHAR(255), -- third-party processor (if applicable)
  purpose TEXT NOT NULL,
  legal_basis VARCHAR(100) NOT NULL, -- 'consent', 'contract', 'legal_obligation', 'legitimate_interest'
  data_categories TEXT[], -- ['contact_info', 'payment_data', 'research_areas']
  data_subjects VARCHAR(100), -- 'members', 'conference_attendees', 'board_members'
  recipients TEXT[], -- who receives the data
  transfer_countries VARCHAR(255), -- for international transfers
  retention_period VARCHAR(255),
  security_measures TEXT,
  dpia_required BOOLEAN DEFAULT false,
  dpia_completed BOOLEAN DEFAULT false,
  created_at TIMESTAMP DEFAULT NOW(),
  updated_at TIMESTAMP DEFAULT NOW()
);

-- User privacy settings (preferences beyond consents)
CREATE TABLE user_privacy_settings (
  user_id UUID PRIMARY KEY REFERENCES users(id) ON DELETE CASCADE,
  email_visibility VARCHAR(20) DEFAULT 'private', -- 'public', 'members', 'board', 'private'
  phone_visibility VARCHAR(20) DEFAULT 'private',
  address_visibility VARCHAR(20) DEFAULT 'private',
  profile_visibility VARCHAR(20) DEFAULT 'members',
  cv_visibility VARCHAR(20) DEFAULT 'members',
  show_conference_history BOOLEAN DEFAULT true,
  show_badges BOOLEAN DEFAULT true,
  allow_photo_tagging BOOLEAN DEFAULT false,
  searchable_in_directory BOOLEAN DEFAULT false,
  updated_at TIMESTAMP DEFAULT NOW()
);

-- Data access requests (GDPR/CCPA requests)
CREATE TABLE data_access_requests (
  id UUID PRIMARY KEY DEFAULT uuid_generate_v4(),
  user_id UUID REFERENCES users(id),
  request_type VARCHAR(50) NOT NULL, -- 'access', 'deletion', 'portability', 'rectification', 'objection'
  status VARCHAR(50) DEFAULT 'pending', -- 'pending', 'verified', 'processing', 'completed', 'denied'
  requested_at TIMESTAMP DEFAULT NOW(),
  verified_at TIMESTAMP,
  completed_at TIMESTAMP,
  denial_reason TEXT,
  verification_token VARCHAR(255),
  export_url TEXT, -- for data access requests
  handled_by UUID REFERENCES users(id), -- admin who handled request
  notes TEXT
);
```
